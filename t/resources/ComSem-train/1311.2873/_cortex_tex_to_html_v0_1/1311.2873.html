A note on real algebraic groups

Hassan Azad Department of Mathematics and Statistics, King Fahd University, Saudi Arabia hassanaz@kfupm.edu.sa  and  Indranil Biswas School of Mathematics, Tata Institute of Fundamental Research, Homi Bhabha Road, Bombay 400005, India indranil@math.tifr.res.in
Abstract.

The efficacy of using complexifications to understand the structure of real algebraic groups is demonstrated. In particular the following result is proved. If LLL is an algebraic subgroup of a connected real algebraic group G⊂GL⁢(n,ℝ)GGLnℝG\,\subset\,\mathrm{GL}(n,{\mathbb{R}}) such that the complexification of LLL contains a maximal torus of the complexification of GGG, then LLL contains a Cartan subgroup of GGG.

Key words and phrases:
Real algebraic group, Cartan subgroup, solvable group, group action.

20G20, 22E15, 14L30

1. Introduction

The usefulness of the point of view of algebraic groups for understanding Lie groups is demonstrated in the book of Onishchik–Vinberg [OV]. In this note we prove, in the same spirit, some results on real algebraic groups.

If one uses this point of view, many results in real Lie groups follow easily from known results in complex algebraic groups-for example the existence of Cartan subgroups and the structure of real parabolic subgroups.

Our main result is the following (see Proposition 8):

Let LLL be an algebraic subgroup of a connected real algebraic group G⊂GL⁢(n,ℝ)Gnormal-GLnℝG\,\subset\,\mathrm{GL}(n,{\mathbb{R}}) such that the complexification LℂsuperscriptLℂL^{\mathbb{C}} of LLL contains a maximal torus of the complexification GℂsuperscriptGℂG^{\mathbb{C}} of GGG. Then LLL contains a Cartan subgroup of GGG.

The following geometric result is also proved (see Proposition 7):

Let GGG be a solvable real algebraic group whose eigenvalues are all real. For any algebraic action of GℂsuperscriptGℂG^{\mathbb{C}} on a complex variety, any compact orbit of GGG is a point.

Recall that a subalgebra of a complex semisimple Lie algebra is a Cartan subalgebra if it is maximal abelian and represented in the adjoint representation by semisimple endomorphisms [He, p. 162]. This coincides with the definition of Cartan subalgebras given in e.g [Kn, p. 133], as shown in [Kn, p. 135]. In this paper, a connected subgroup CCC of a real algebraic group GGG is a Cartan subgroup if its complexification CℂsuperscriptCℂC^{\mathbb{C}} in the complexification GℂsuperscriptGℂG^{\mathbb{C}} of GGG is a maximal algebraic torus in the sense of [OV, p. 133].

2. Real algebraic groups

The reader is referred to [OV] for general facts about real algebraic groups. However, for technical reasons, we find it more convenient to modify the definition of real algebraic groups as follows:

A connected subgroup G⊂GL⁢(n,ℝ)GGLnℝG\,\subset\,\text{GL}(n,{\mathbb{R}}) will be called a real algebraic group if the connected subgroup of GL⁢(n,ℂ)GLnℂ\text{GL}(n,{\mathbb{C}}) whose Lie algebra of Lie⁢(G)+-1⋅Lie⁢(G)LieGnormal-⋅1LieG\text{Lie}(G)+\sqrt{-1}\cdot\text{Lie}(G) is an algebraic subgroup of GL⁢(n,ℂ)GLnℂ\text{GL}(n,{\mathbb{C}}).

The Lie algebra Lie⁢(G)LieG\text{Lie}(G) will be denoted by 𝔤𝔤\mathfrak{g}. The above connected subgroup of GL⁢(n,ℂ)GLnℂ\text{GL}(n,{\mathbb{C}}) with Lie algebra 𝔤+-1⋅𝔤𝔤normal-⋅1𝔤{\mathfrak{g}}+\sqrt{-1}\cdot{\mathfrak{g}} will be denoted by GℂsuperscriptGℂG^{\mathbb{C}}.

The group GℂsuperscriptGℂG^{\mathbb{C}} is generated by the complex one-parameter subgroups {exp⁡(z⁢X)∣ z∈ℂ}conditional-setzXzℂ\{\exp(zX)\,\mid\,z\,\in\,{\mathbb{C}}\}, X∈𝔤X𝔤X\,\in\,\mathfrak{g}.

The connected component, containing the identity element, of the Zariski closure of GGG is GℂsuperscriptGℂG^{\mathbb{C}}, and the connected component, containing the identity element, of the set of real points of GℂsuperscriptGℂG^{\mathbb{C}} is GGG.

We will call the set of real points of a Zariski closure of a subset as the real Zariski closure of the subset.

We have also included details that were not given in the earlier paper [AB] on solvable real algebraic groups which have all positive eigenvalues. Classical references for this subject are the papers of Mostow [Mo] and Matsumoto [Ma].

One of the main technical tools is the following version of Lie’s theorem for real solvable groups. Recall that, by definition, a real algebraic group is connected.

Lemma 3.

Any real unipotent element uuu of GℂsuperscriptGℂG^{\mathbb{C}} is in GGG.

Corollary 4.

If g∈GℂgsuperscriptGℂg\,\in\,G^{\mathbb{C}} is real and has positive eigenvalues, then ggg is in GGG.

Proof.

The semisimple and unipotent parts of ggg are in GℂsuperscriptGℂG^{\mathbb{C}} and are real. Therefore, by Lemma 2 and Lemma 3, they are in GGG. Hence ggg is in GGG. ∎

Corollary 5.

The real Zaraski closure of any subgroup of GℂsuperscriptGℂG^{\mathbb{C}} generated by real elements with positive eigenvalues is connected and is contained in GGG.

Proposition 8.

Corollary 9.

Every semisimple element of Lie⁢(G)normal-LieG\mathrm{Lie}(G) lies in some Cartan subalgebra of Lie⁢(G)normal-LieG\mathrm{Lie}(G).

Proof.

Let XXX be a real semisimple element of Lie⁢(G)LieG\mathrm{Lie}(G). Then XXX is tangent to the connected component HHH of the real Zariski closure of the one-parameter subgroup generated by XXX. Therefore, as the centralizer Z⁢(Hℂ)ZsuperscriptHℂZ(H^{\mathbb{C}}) of HℂsuperscriptHℂH^{\mathbb{C}} in GℂsuperscriptGℂG^{\mathbb{C}} contains a maximal torus of GℂsuperscriptGℂG^{\mathbb{C}}, we conclude that the centralizer Z⁢(H)ZHZ(H) of HHH in GGG contains a Cartan subgroup, say CCC, of GGG. But then C⁢HCHCH is a connected group and the connected component, containing the identity element, of its real Zariski closure consists of semisimple elements. Therefore, C⁢H=CCHCCH\,=\,C and HHH is contained in CCC. Consequently, XXX is in the Lie algebra of CCC. ∎

Another consequence of Proposition 8 is the following result of [Mo].

Proposition 10 ([Mo]).

Let PPP be a subgroup of a real semisimple group GGG with PℂsuperscriptPℂP^{\mathbb{C}} a parabolic subgroup of GℂsuperscriptGℂG^{\mathbb{C}}. Then PPP contains a noncompact Cartan subgroup of GGG.

Proof.

By Proposition 8, the above subgroup PPP contains a Cartan subgroup, say CCC, of GGG. If CCC is compact, then, as PℂsuperscriptPℂP^{\mathbb{C}} is conjugation invariant, and conjugation maps any root to its negative, the unipotent radical of PℂsuperscriptPℂP^{\mathbb{C}} contains a subgroup whose Lie algebra is sl⁢(2,ℂ)sl2ℂ\text{sl}(2,{\mathbb{C}}). This contradiction proves that CCC must contain a closed real diagonalizable subgroup. ∎

As shown in Proposition 10, the parabolic subgroup PPP has a noncompact Cartan subgroup. We can write it as A⁢CACAC, where AAA is split and CCC compact: by Proposition 1, both AAA and CCC are real algebraic.

Now we take a basis of the Lie algebra of A⁢CACAC, with the basis of AAA coming before CCC. Under conjugation, and with this lexicographic ordering of the roots, conjugation maps every root of Z⁢(A)ZAZ(A) – the centralizer of AAA – into a negative, while positive roots supported outside the simple system of roots of AAA are preserved.

Since centralizers of semisimple connected groups in a complex group are Levi-complements, one can obtain in this way the structure of real parabolic subgroups as well as a fine classification of maximal solvable subgroups, starting from the existence of a real diagonalizable torus – as shown in Matsumoto [Ma] and Mostow [Mo].

Other, more recent applications of the real algebraic groups are given in [Ch].
