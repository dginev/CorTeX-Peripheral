The Weierstrass semigroups on double covers of genus two curves

Takeshi Harui Academic Support Center, Kogakuin University Hachioji, 192-0015, Japan Jiryo Komeda Department of Mathematics, Center for Basic Education and Integrated Learning Kanagawa Institute of Technology, Atsugi, 243-0292, Japan and Akira Ohbuchi Department of Mathematics, Faculty of Integrated Arts and Sciences Tokushima University, Tokushima, 770-8502, Japan E-mail: takeshi@cwo.zaq.ne.jp, kt13459@ns.kogakuin.ac.jpE-mail: komeda@gen.kanagawa-it.ac.jpE-mail: ohbuchi@tokushima-u.ac.jp
We show that three numerical semigroups ⟨5,6,7,8⟩, ⟨3,7,8⟩ and ⟨3,5⟩ are of double covering type, i.e., the Weierstrass semigroups of ramification points on double covers of curves. Combining the result with [5] and [2] we can determine the Weierstrass semigroups of the ramification points on double covers of genus two curves. 2010 Mathematics Subject Classification: 14H55, 14H45, 20M14 Key words: Numerical semigroup, Weierstrass semigroup, Double cover of a curve, Curve of genus two

Introduction1

Let C be a complete nonsingular irreducible curve over an algebraically closed field k of characteristic 0, which is called a curve in this paper. For a point P of C, we set
H(P)={α∈ℕ0| there exists a rational function f on C with (f)∞=αP},
which is called the Weierstrass semigroup of P where ℕ0 denotes the additive monoid of non-negative integers. A submonoid H of ℕ0 is called a numerical semigroup if its complement ℕ0\H is a finite set. The cardinality of ℕ0\H is called the genus of H, which is denoted by g(H). It is known that the Weierstrass semigroup of a point on a curve of genus g is a numerical semigroup of genus g. For a numerical semigroup H~ we denote by d2(H~) the set of consisting of the elements h~/2 with even h~∈H~, which becomes a numerical semigroup. A numerical semigroup H~ is said to be of double covering type if there exists a double covering π:C~⟶C of a curve with a ramification point P~ over P satisfying H(P~)=H~. In this case we have d2(H(P~))=H(P).
We are interested in numerical semigroups of double covering type. Let H~ be a numerical semigroup of genus g~ with d2(H~)=ℕ0 whose genus is 0. Then the semigroup H~ is ⟨2,2g~+1⟩, where for any positive integers a1,a2,…,an we denote by ⟨a1,a2,…,an⟩ the additive monoid generated by a1,a2,…,an. In this case H~ is the Weierstrass semigroup of a ramification point P~ on a double cover of the projective line which is of genus g~. Hence, H~ is of double covering type.
Let H~ be a numerical semigroup of genus g~ with d2(H~)=⟨2,3⟩ which is the only one numerical semigroup of genus 1. Then the semigroup H~ is either ⟨3,4,5⟩ or ⟨3,4⟩ or ⟨4,5,6,7⟩ or ⟨4,6,2g~-3⟩ with g~≧4 or ⟨4,6,2g~-1,2g~+1⟩ with g~≧4. We can show that there is a double covering of an elliptic curve with a ramification point whose Weierstrass semigroup is any semigroup in the above ones ( for example, see [1], [2] ).
Oliveira and Pimentel [5] studied the semigroup H~=⟨6,8,10,n⟩ with an odd number n≧11. They showed that the semigroup H~ is of double covering type. In this case we have d2(H~)=⟨3,4,5⟩, which is of genus 2. Moreover, in [2] we proved that any numerical semigroup H~ with d2(H~)=⟨3,4,5⟩ except ⟨5,6,7,8⟩, ⟨3,7,8⟩, ⟨3,5⟩ and ⟨3,5,7⟩ is of double covering type. In view of the fact that g(⟨3,5,7⟩)=3<2⋅2 the semigroup ⟨3,5,7⟩ is not of double covering type. There is another numerical semigroup of genus 2, which is ⟨2,5⟩. Using the result of Main Theorem in [4] every numerical semigroup H~ with d2(H~)=⟨2,5⟩ is of double covering type. In this paper we will study the remaining three numerical semigroups. Namely we prove the following: Theorem 1 The three numerical semigroups ⟨5,6,7,8⟩, ⟨3,7,8⟩ and ⟨3,5⟩ are of double covering type. Combining this theorem with the results in [5] and [2], we have the following conclusion: Theorem 2 Let H~ be a numerical semigroup with g(d2(H~))=2. If H~≠⟨3,5,7⟩, then it is of double covering type.

The proof of Theorem2

To prove that the three numerical semigroups are of double covering type we use the following remark which is stated in Theorem 2.2 of [3]. Remark 1. Let H~ be a numerical semigroup. We set
n=min{h~∈H~∣h~ is odd} and g(H~)=2g(d2(H~))+n-12-r
with some non-negative integer r. Assume that H=d2(H~) is Weierstrass. Take a pointed curve (C,P) with H(P)=H. Let Q1,…,Qr be points of C different from P with h0(Q1+⋯+Qr)=1. Moreover, assume that H~ has an expression
H~=2H+⟨n,n+2l1,…,n+2ls⟩
of generators with positive integers l1,…,ls such that
h0(liP+Q1+⋯+Qr)=h0((li-1)P+Q1+⋯+Qr)+1
for all i. If the divisor nP-2Q1-⋯-2Qr is linearly equivalent to some reduced divisor not containing P, then there is a double covering π:C~⟶C with a ramification point P~ over P satisfying H(P~)=H~, hence H~ is of double covering type. By seeing the proof of Theorem 2.2 in [3] we may replace the assumption in Theorem 2.2 in [3] that the complete linear system |nP-2Q1-⋯-2Qr| is base point free by the above assumption that the divisor nP-2Q1-⋯-2Qr is linearly equivalent to some reduced divisor not containing P. Case 1. Let H~=⟨5,6,7,8⟩. Then we have H=d2(H~)=⟨3,4,5⟩ and g(H~)=5=2⋅2+5-12-1. Moreover, we have H~=2H+⟨5,5+2⋅1⟩. Let C be a curve of genus 2 and ι the hyperelliptic involution on C. Let us take a point P of C with H(P)=⟨3,4,5⟩ and 3(P-ι(P))≁0. Then we get h0(P+ι(P))=2=h0(ι(P))+1. Moreover, we have R≠P if the complete linear system |5P-2ι(P)| has a base point R. Indeed, we assume that R=P. Then we have
h0(5P-2ι(P)-P)=h0(5P-2ι(P))=3+1-2=2,
which implies that
4P-2ι(P)∼g21∼P+ι(P).
Hence, we get 3(P-ι(P))∼0. This is a contradiction.
We assume that |5P-2ι(P)| has a base point R. Then we get 5P-2ι(P)∼R+E, where E is an effective divisor of degree 2 with projective dimension 1. In this case the complete linear system |E| is base point free. Therefore, the divisor 5P-2ι(P) is linearly equivalent to some reduced divisor not containing P. If |5P-2ι(P)| is base point free, then the divisor 5P-2ι(P) satisfies the above condition. By Remark 1 the semigroup H~=⟨5,6,7,8⟩ is of double covering type. Case 2. Let H~=⟨3,7,8⟩. Then we have H=d2(H~)=⟨3,4,5⟩ and g(H~)=4=2⋅2+3-12-1. Moreover, we have H~=2H+⟨3,3+2⋅2⟩. Let C be a curve of genus 2 and ι the hyperelliptic involution on C. We take a point P of C with H(P)=⟨3,4,5⟩. Let φ:C⟶ℙ1 be a covering of degree 3 corresponding to the complete linear system |3P|. We may take the pointed curve (C,P) such that φ has a simple ramification point Q. Then there is another simple ramification point of φ by Riemann-Hurwitz formula. Hence, we may assume that ιP≠Q, which implies that P+Q≁g21. Thus, we get h0(2P+Q)=2=h0(P+Q)+1. Let R be the point satisfying 2Q+R∼3P. Then we have R≠P and 3P-2Q∼R. By Remark 1 the semigroup H~=⟨3,7,8⟩ is of double covering type. Case 3. Let H~=⟨3,5⟩. Then we have H=d2(H~)=⟨3,4,5⟩ and g(H~)=4=2⋅2+3-12-1. Moreover, we have H~=2H+⟨3,3+2⋅1⟩. Let C be a curve whose function field is k(x,y) with an equation y3=(x-c1)(x-c2)(x-c3)2, where c1, c2 and c3 are distinct elements of k. Let π:C⟶ℙ1 be the morphism corresponding to the inclusion k(x)⊂k(x,y). Then C is of genus 2. Let P=P1, P2, P3 and P4 be the ramification points of π. Since π is a cyclic covering, it induces an automorphism σ of C with C/⟨σ⟩≅ℙ1. Let ι be the hyperelliptic involution on C. Then we have σ∘ι=ι∘σ. Indeed, we have
(σ∘ι∘σ-1)∘(σ∘ι∘σ-1)=σ∘ι∘ι∘σ-1=σ∘σ-1=id.
Hence, the automorphism σ∘ι∘σ-1 is an involution. Moreover, we have a bijective correspondence between the sets Fix (ι) and Fix (σ∘ι∘σ-1) sending Q to σ(Q), where Fix (ι) and Fix (σ∘ι∘σ-1) are the sets of the fixed points by ι and σ∘ι∘σ-1 respectively. Hence, σ∘ι∘σ-1 is also the hyperelliptic involution. Thus, we have σ∘ι∘σ-1=ι. Since σ(ι(P))=ι(σ(P))=ι(P), the point ι(P) is a fixed point of σ. Hence, we may assume that ι(P)=P2. Then we obtain h0(P+P2)=2=h0(P2)+1. Moreover, we have
3P-2P2∼3P2-2P2=P2.
By Remark 1 the semigroup ⟨3,5⟩ is of double covering type. □
