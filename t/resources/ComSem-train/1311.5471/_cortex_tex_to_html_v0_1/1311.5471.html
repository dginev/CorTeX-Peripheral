Abstract.

1. Introduction

The by now classical theorem of Ramsey [11] states that no matter how one colors the edges of the large enough complete graph KnsubscriptKnK_{n} with two colors, say red and blue, there will always be a monochromatic copy of KtsubscriptKtK_{t} in it. The smallest such nnn is called the Ramsey number, denoted by r⁢(t)rtr(t) or r⁢(Kt)rsubscriptKtr(K_{t}). First lower and upper bounds on r⁢(t)rtr(t) were obtained by Erdős and Szekeres  [8]: r⁢(t)≤(2⁢t-2t-1)rtbinomial2t2t1r(t)\leq\tbinom{2t-2}{t-1} and by Erdős [6]: r⁢(t)≥2t/2rtsuperscript2t2r(t)\geq 2^{t/2}. Despite numerous efforts by various researchers, the best lower and upper bounds remain asymptotically 2(1+o⁢(1))⁢t/2superscript21o1t22^{(1+o(1))t/2} and 2(1+o⁢(1))⁢2⁢tsuperscript21o12t2^{(1+o(1))2t}, for the currently best bounds see Conlon [5] and Spencer [12].

Thus, one turned to the study of Ramsey numbers of graphs other than complete graphs KtsubscriptKtK_{t}. The multicolor Ramsey number for kkk colors of a graph FFF, denoted rk⁢(F)subscriptrkFr_{k}(F), is defined as the smallest number nnn such that in any coloring of E⁢(Kn)EsubscriptKnE(K_{n}) by kkk colors there is a monochromatic copy of a graph FFF in one of the kkk colors. Much attention was drawn by the conjectures of Burr and Erdős [2] about Ramsey numbers of graphs FFF whose maximum degree is bounded by a constant and which are ddd-degenerate for some constant ddd stating that these Ramsey numbers are linear in v⁢(F):=|V⁢(F)|assignvFVFv(F):=|V(F)|. While the first conjecture has been resolved positively by Chvatál, Rödl, Szemerédi and Trotter [4], the latter one is still open and the best bound is due to Fox and Sudakov [10] being r2⁢(F)≤2cd⁢log⁡n⁢nsubscriptr2Fsuperscript2subscriptcdnnr_{2}(F)\leq 2^{c_{d}\sqrt{\log n}}n for cdsubscriptcdc_{d} depending on ddd only.

A related conjecture of Erdős and Graham [7] states that among all graphs FFF with m=(t2)mbinomialt2m=\tbinom{t}{2} edges and no isolated vertices the Ramsey number r⁢(t)rtr(t) of the complete graph KtsubscriptKtK_{t} is an upper bound on r2⁢(F)subscriptr2Fr_{2}(F). A relaxation conjectured by Erdős [3] states that at least r⁢(F)≤2c⁢mrFsuperscript2cmr(F)\leq 2^{c\sqrt{m}} should hold for any graph FFF with mmm edges and no isolated vertices and some absolute constant ccc. This was verified by Alon, Krivelevich and Sudakov [1] who showed that if FFF is bipartite, has mmm edges and no isolated vertices then r⁢(F)≤216⁢m+1rFsuperscript216m1r(F)\leq 2^{16\sqrt{m}+1}, and for nonbipartite FFF showing r⁢(F)≤27⁢m⁢log2⁡mrFsuperscript27msubscript2mr(F)\leq 2^{7\sqrt{m}\log_{2}m}. Finally, the general case was settled by Sudakov [14] who proved r⁢(F)≤2250⁢mrFsuperscript2250mr(F)\leq 2^{250\sqrt{m}}.

In his concluding remarks in [14], Sudakov mentions that the methods used to settle the general case are not extendible to more colors and it would be interesting to understand the growth of rk⁢(F)subscriptrkFr_{k}(F). It is easy to see that there is an upper bound on rk⁢(F)subscriptrkFr_{k}(F) of the form kk⁢v⁢(F)superscriptkkvFk^{kv(F)} by finding a monochromatic copy of K2⁢m⊃FFsubscriptK2mK_{2m}\supset F using the classical color focussing argument. In this note we prove to the best of our knowledge a first nontrivial upper bound rk⁢(F)≤k6⁢k⁢m2/3subscriptrkFsuperscriptk6ksuperscriptm23r_{k}(F)\leq k^{6km^{2/3}}.

Let FFF be a graph with mmm edges and no isolated vertices. Then, for k≥3k3k\geq 3 it holds

rk⁢(F)≤k3⋅2-1/3⁢k⁢m2/3+k⁢(2⁢m)1/3⁢8⁢m.subscriptrkFsuperscriptknormal-⋅3superscript213ksuperscriptm23ksuperscript2m138mr_{k}(F)\leq k^{3\cdot 2^{-1/3}km^{2/3}+k(2m)^{1/3}}8m.

Let FFF be a bipartite graph with mmm edges and no isolated vertices. Then, for k≥2k2k\geq 2 it holds

rk⁢(F)≤26⁢m3/2⁢k2⁢k⁢m+1/2.subscriptrkFsuperscript26superscriptm32superscriptk2km12r_{k}(F)\leq 2^{6}m^{3/2}k^{2\sqrt{km}+1/2}.

Remarkably, this upper bound is asymptotically the “same” as the upper bound 2(1+o⁢(1))⁢2⁢ksuperscript21o12k2^{(1+o(1))2k} for r⁢(k)rkr(k) with m=(k2)mbinomialk2m=\tbinom{k}{2}.

The methods we use are slight modifications of the arguments of Fox and Sudakov from [9] and of Alon, Krivelevich and Sudakov [1]. The paper is organized as follows. 4. Multicolor Ramsey number of general graphs with mmm edges

In this section we heavily rely on the tools developed by Fox and Sudakov in [9]. There they showed that rk⁢(F)≤k2⁢k⁢Δ⁢q⁢nsubscriptrkFsuperscriptk2knormal-Δqnr_{k}(F)\leq k^{2k\Delta q}n for a graph FFF with nnn vertices, Δ⁢(F)=Δnormal-ΔFnormal-Δ\Delta(F)=\Delta and χ⁢(F)=qχFq\chi(F)=q (more generally, it holds for kkk not necessarily isomorphic graphs F1subscriptF1F_{1}, …, FksubscriptFkF_{k} with the same properties). Our proof strategy is in fact a slight modification of their argument intertwined with the process of first embedding high degree vertices. The idea of embedding high degree vertices already occurs in [1]. More precisely, since we are given a general graph FFF with mmm edges, we first seek to embed vertices of high degree (which will be done in a similar way as in the proof of Theorem 2). However, this time we are going to use Lemma 3 instead of Theorem 5 repeatedly. The authors in [9] show rk⁢(F)≤k2⁢k⁢Δ⁢q⁢nsubscriptrkFsuperscriptk2knormal-Δqnr_{k}(F)\leq k^{2k\Delta q}n by applying iteratively Lemma 3 roughly q⁢kqkqk times, “loosing” each time roughly a factor of k-k⁢Δsuperscriptkknormal-Δk^{-k\Delta}. Afterwards one identifies a long enough nested sequence to perform embedding (Lemma 4). In our case however, we first need to reduce the maximum degree of FFF, and only then we will apply Lemma 3. However, its applications intertwine with the focussing argument similar to the previous section, as each color might get filled up differently quickly.
