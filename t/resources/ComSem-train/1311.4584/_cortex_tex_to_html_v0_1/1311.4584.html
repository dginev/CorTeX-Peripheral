Abstract.

We give a simple example of a countable metric space that does not embed bi-Lipschitz with distortion strictly less than 2 into any Asplund space.

1. Introduction

We say that a Banach space XXX is DDD-bi-Lipschitz universal if every separable metric space embeds into XXX with distortion at most DDD. The results of [5], resp. [1], show that c0subscriptc0c_{0} is 222-bi-Lipschitz universal, resp. is not DDD-bi-Lipschitz universal for any D<2D2D<2. In the recent preprint [3], F. Baudier raised the following question: given a C⁢(K)CKC(K) Banach space XXX, what is the least constant DDD such that XXX is DDD-bi-Lipschitz universal?

The goal of the present article is to prove that Asplund C⁢(K)CKC(K) spaces and, more generally, all Asplund Banach spaces can be DDD-bi-Lipschitz universal only if D≥2D2D\geq 2.

We obtain our C⁢(K)CKC(K) result as a slight modification of Baudier’s elaboration on Aharoni’s original argument that ℓ1subscriptnormal-ℓ1\ell_{1} does not embed into c0subscriptc0c_{0} with distortion strictly less than 222. The general result is then obtained by a direct application of the deep “Zippin’s lemma” [7, Theorem 1.2].

An immediate corollary of our C⁢(K)CKC(K) result is that the C⁢(K)CKC(K) spaces which are DDD-bi-Lipschitz universal with D<2D2D<2 are isomorphically universal for the class of separable Banach spaces. One may ask whether this is true in general.

Problem 1.

Assume that XXX is DDD-bi-Lipschitz universal with D<2D2D<2. Does then every separable Banach space linearly embed into XXX?

Corollary 3.

If X=C⁢(K)XCKX=C(K) for some metrizable compact space KKK and (M,d)Md(M,d) embeds into XXX with distortion strictly less than 222, then XXX is (isomorphically) universal for all separable spaces.

Proof.

Using Lemma 2 and Milutin’s theorem [6, 2], XXX is isomorphic to C⁢[0,1]C01C[0,1]. Thus, Banach-Mazur’s theorem [2] finishes the proof. ∎

Theorem 4.

Let XXX be an Asplund space and assume that (M,d)Md(M,d) embeds into XXX with distortion DDD. Then D≥2D2D\geq 2. Consequently, no Asplund space is universal for embeddings of distortion strictly less than 222 for all separable metric spaces.
