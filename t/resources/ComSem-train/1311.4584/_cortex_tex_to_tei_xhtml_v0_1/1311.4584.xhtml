Low distortion embeddings into Asplund Banach spaces

Abstract.
We give a simple example of a countable metric space that does not embed bi-Lipschitz with distortion strictly less than 2 into any Asplund space.

Introduction1.

We say that a Banach space X is D-bi-Lipschitz universal if every separable metric space embeds into X with distortion at most D. The results of [5], resp. [1], show that c0 is 2-bi-Lipschitz universal, resp. is not D-bi-Lipschitz universal for any D<2. In the recent preprint [3], F. Baudier raised the following question: given a C(K) Banach space X, what is the least constant D such that X is D-bi-Lipschitz universal?
The goal of the present article is to prove that Asplund C(K) spaces and, more generally, all Asplund Banach spaces can be D-bi-Lipschitz universal only if D≥2.
We obtain our C(K) result as a slight modification of Baudier 's elaboration on Aharoni 's original argument that ℓ1 does not embed into c0 with distortion strictly less than 2. The general result is then obtained by a direct application of the deep " Zippin 's lemma " [7, Theorem 1.2].
An immediate corollary of our C(K) result is that the C(K) spaces which are D-bi-Lipschitz universal with D<2 are isomorphically universal for the class of separable Banach spaces. One may ask whether this is true in general.
.Problem 1
Assume that X is D-bi-Lipschitz universal with D<2. Does then every separable Banach space linearly embed into X?
The notation we use is standard. A mapping f:M→N between metric spaces (M,d) and (N,ρ) is bi-Lipschitz if there are constants C1,C2>0 such that C1d(x,y)≤ρ(f(x),f(y))≤C2d(x,y) for all x,y∈M. The distortion dist(f) of f is defined as infC2C1 where the infimum is taken over all constants C1,C2 which satisfy the above inequality. We say that M embeds bi-Lipschitz into N with distortion D if there exists such f:M→N with dist(f)=D. In this case, if the target space N is a Banach space, we may always assume ( by changing f ) that C1=1. For the following notions and results, see [4]. A Banach space X is called Asplund if every closed separable subspace Y⊂X has separable dual. A Hausdorff compact K is called scattered if there exists an ordinal α such that the Cantor-Bendixson derivative K(α) is empty. A countable Hausdorff compact is necessarily scattered. If K is a Hausdorff compact then C(K) is Asplund iff K is scattered.

.Lemma 2
Let X=C(K) for some compact space K and assume that there exist D∈[1,2) and f:M→X such that
d(x,y)≤∥f(x)-f(y)∥≤Dd(x,y).
Then K is not scattered.
Proof.
We may assume, without loss of generality, that f(∅)=0. We will show for any ordinal α that K(α)≠∅. Let η=4-2D>0. For any i,j∈ℕ let Xi,j={x*∈K:|⟨x*,f(i)-f(j)⟩|≥η}. These are closed subsets of K. We will show that for any disjoint A,B∈F and any ordinal α we have
⋂a∈A,b∈BXa,b∩K(α)≠∅.
Let us start with α=0. Let A,B∈F be disjoint. We take x*∈K such that |⟨x*,f(A)-f(B)⟩|=∥f(A)-f(B)∥≥4. Then for any a∈A and any b∈B we have
|⟨x*,f(a)-f(b)⟩|≥|⟨x*,f(A)-f(B)⟩|-|⟨x*,f(A)-f(a)⟩|-|⟨x*,f(B)-f(b)⟩|≥4-2D=η
thus x*∈⋂a∈A,b∈BXa,b∩K.
Let us assume that we have proved the claim for every β<α. If α is a limit ordinal then the closedness of Xi,j implies the claim. Let us assume that α=β+1. Let us fix two disjoint sets A,B∈F. Let N=1+maxA∪B. By the inductive hypothesis we know that for N≤i<j there is x*i,j∈K s.t.
x*i,j∈K(β)∩⋂a∈A∪{i},b∈B∪{j}Xa,b.
We put Γ:={x*i,j:N≤i<j}⊂K. We define Φ:ℕ∩[N,∞)→ℓ∞(Γ) by Φ(i):=(⟨γ,f(i)⟩)γ∈Γ. Then the image of Φ is an η-separated countably infinite bounded set. Indeed ∥Φ(i)∥∞≤∥f(i)∥≤Dd(i,∅)=D. Let N≤i<j then ∥Φ(i)-Φ(j)∥∞≥|⟨x*i,j,f(i)-f(j)⟩|≥η. Thus Γ is infinite and therefore K(β)∩⋂a∈A,b∈BXa,b is infinite, too. Now the closedness of ⋂a∈A,b∈BXa,b and compactness of K(β) imply our claim. ourQED
.Corollary 3
If X=C(K) for some metrizable compact space K and (M,d) embeds into X with distortion strictly less than 2, then X is ( isomorphically ) universal for all separable spaces.
Proof.
Using Lemma 2 and Milutin 's theorem [6, 2], X is isomorphic to C[0,1]. Thus, Banach-Mazur 's theorem [2] finishes the proof. ourQED
.Theorem 4
Let X be an Asplund space and assume that (M,d) embeds into X with distortion D. Then D≥2. Consequently, no Asplund space is universal for embeddings of distortion strictly less than 2 for all separable metric spaces.
Since M is countable, we may assume that X is separable. The proof is then based on Lemma 2 and the following theorem of Zippin, see [7, Theorem 1.2] or [6, Lemma 5.11].
.Theorem 5
Let X be a separable Asplund space. Let ε>0. Then there exist a Banach space Z, a countable Hausdorff ( in particular scattered ) compact S, a subspace Y of Z isometric to C(S) and a linear embedding i:X→Z with ∥i∥∥i-1∥<1+ε such that for any x∈X we have
distZ(i(x),Y)≤ε∥i(x)∥Z.
Proof of Theorem 4.
Let us assume that D<2. Let ε>0 be small enough so that D′=D(1+ε)<2 and also that for η:=ε2D′ we have 1+4ε1-2ηD′<2. Then (M,d) embeds into Z with distortion D′<2 via some embedding g such that d(x,y)≤∥g(x)-g(y)∥≤D′d(x,y). We may assume, without loss of generality, g(∅)=0. Thus for every x∈M we have ∥g(x)∥≤2D′. We know that for each x∈M there is f(x)∈Y such that ∥g(x)-f(x)∥≤η. This implies that ∥g(x)-g(y)∥-2η≤∥f(x)-f(y)∥≤∥g(x)-g(y)∥+2η. Now since 1≤d(x,y) we have
d(x,y)(1-2η)≤∥f(x)-f(y)∥≤d(x,y)D′(1+4ε).
This proves that f is a bi-Lipschitz embedding of M into C(S) with distortion strictly less than 2 which is impossible according to Lemma 2. ourQED
